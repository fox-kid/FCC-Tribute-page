Agatha Christie

"Duchess of Death", the "Mistress of Mystery", and the "Queen of Crime"

List of Agatha Christie's life and accomplishements:

  • Her works put her third on the all-time bestsellers list, after the Bible and Shakespeare. Her books have been sold in more than 2 billion copies, and still counting!
  • While being born in an upper-middle class family, she has never received formal education
  • Christie is the only crime writer to have created two equally famous protagonists: Hercule Poirot and Miss Jane Marple
  • She once disappeared for 10 days and was found in Swan Hydropathic Hotel in Harrogate, with amnesia regarding to that event, registered under the name of her husband's lover
  • She was shy, disliked loud noices and crowds, gramophones and cinema, the taste of alcohol and cigarettes. She was much more into the tranquil plesures of life
  • One of her private pleasures was gardening; She has won local prizes for horticulture
  • She has wrote and published sixty-six detective novels and fourteen short story collections
  • Her stage play The Mousetrap holds the world record for longest initial run
  • She has published six romantic novels under her pen name - Mary Westmacott. For almost two decades the public had no idea it was her
  • Christie was accussed of anti-Semitic and rasist remarks in her works because of depicting Jews and black people with negative traits as if they were characteristic to them. Anti-Defamation League penned a letter objecting to her apparent anti-Semitism, so her agent gave permission to her U.S. publishers to delete any distasteful passages about Jews and Catholics
  • She started writing mysteries as a challenge her sister gave her, who said she probably couldn't write a good one

'She was a shy person: she disliked public appearances: but she was friendly and sharp-witted to meet. By inclination as well as breeding she belonged to the English upper middle-class. She wrote about, and for, people like herself. That was an essential part of her charm.'

The Times. 13 January 1976.
